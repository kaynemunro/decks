What we found · downsides

What went the wrong way.

Churn

Voluntary churn up — port-out-led

Voluntary churn rose +4%; 2degrees took ≈46% of ports. Likely drivers: their phone discounting (our −$500 S26 gap) and our price rise still flowing through — sending price-sensitive customers to the cheapest plans in market.

Timing

June boosts hit the bill wave — and hurt

Same boost as April — the moment changed: April rode the price-rise comms window; June landed on the first raised bills. +0.89pp worse than control on price-risen segments.

Media

Media mix backwards

Search (38.6×) trimmed; VOD (0×) up-weighted — and the paid CTA was re-pointed from WhatsInMyWallet to plan pages, breaking conversion tracking.

Wallets

Wallet creation — the CTA re-point

The marketing call-to-action was re-pointed away from whatsinmywallet.one.nz — sending paid traffic away from where wallets get created (and tracked).

What we learned

Six lessons we'll keep.

1

Reward the multi-line home — grow without the A&R hit.

Our most effective base growth came from rewarding multi-line connection with earned value — none of it hits A&R in the month.

2

Ride the comms window, never the bill wave.

At-risk boosts worked in the initial comms window; after the bills landed we needed to avoid the high-risk impacted base — still effective for low & mid risk.

3

The Growth Fund works when it's used.

Sylvia Park activated it and grew acquisition significantly; Albany largely didn't — and didn't grow.

4

Execution and adoption win on the day.

5

Stay competitive on the key offers.

We were too far out of market on the S26 (−$500 to Spark).

6

Run it again in February–March.

Base growth at the FY close — without the A&R impact, timed to a comms window.

The quality of the growth

The best growth month of the half — and it’s only going to get better.

June’s growth wasn’t just the biggest of the half — it was the best quality. Multi-product sales grew from 39% to 48% of IFP purchases through the month, and the more connected the home, the longer they stay with us.
