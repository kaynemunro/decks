Why monthly

It's easier to run — and it's what the review already recommended

Less admin, fewer errors

Weekly balances proved cumbersome to monitor and manage — the review's own words. Set the target and the fund once a month and most of the moving parts disappear.

It stops us over-paying

Paying weekly wins while ignoring down-weeks cost ~2.3× the store-level lift — $18,300 earned against ~$8k of true store value. One monthly reconciliation nets that out.

The one thing we protect

Sylvia Park's win was partly built on the weekly buzz — tracking and gamifying every sale. So we keep the weekly scoreboard and move only the pay settlement to monthly. Reps still see progress every week; they just aren't re-cut every week.

The monthly pot · recommended initial load

$600 to open — sized to a month of closes

Reps deploy Phone Dollars by plan tier to close a deal: $100 (Small), $200 (Medium), $300 (One Plan). The initial load should cover a realistic month of those closes — then the earn rate tops it up.

How we size it

Expected fund-assisted closes per month × the blended plan value. Engaged trial reps used the fund ~4–6 times a month; a blended deploy sits around $200 (mostly Medium/One Plan on the acquisition deals we want). 4–6 × ~$200 ≈ $500–$1,000.

The recommendation

$600 standard initial load, tiered by store volume: $750–$900 high-volume large-format, $400–$500 smaller-format, floor $300. Then +$300 earned per acquisition above baseline tops the fund up so strong reps never run dry. Per-agent cap ~$1,500/month.

Why $600, not the trial's $300

The trial opened reps at $300 for a 3-week window and the keen ones ran it down. A full month, fronted up front, needs roughly double — enough for ~one close a week plus a couple of competitive saves — without over-funding the floor. Indicative store pots: a ~13-rep large store ≈ $8–9k; a ~5-rep small store ≈ $2–2.5k; the six together ≈ $25–30k initial load for the month, plus earned top-ups.

Guardrails

Replacing the weekly brake — on purpose

The weekly model had an automatic brake: overspend and you opened lean the next week. Monthly loses that, so we rebuild it explicitly.

1

Hard per-agent cap

~$1,500/month, enforced at the till — a load can't exceed the rep's remaining allocation.

2

Store-level pool cap

The store pot is the sum of rep allocations — so one rep can't drain the store.

3

Spend-pace tripwire

Flags any rep spending faster than the month is elapsing — a coaching signal, never an auto-cut.

Month end

Unspent allocation lapses; the ledger resets clean. We'll put one question to the group: a small recognition for reps who beat target without burning their fund — so we reward the Sylvia Park discipline rather than quietly penalising it.

What we're building

The capability changes, in plain terms

1

Monthly engine

Switch targets, allocation and settlement from weekly to a single monthly cycle. One target, one fronted allocation, one reconciliation at month end. Retire the weekly carry-over machinery.

2

Entitlement gate at point of sale

Only reps on the locked monthly roster can deploy in WhatsInMyWallet — a non-entitled rep simply can't load. This closes the ~$2,160 of non-entitled deployments we saw, at source.

3

Caps, pool & tripwire

Per-agent monthly cap, store-level pool cap, and a mid-month spend-pace flag — the guardrails that replace the weekly brake.

4

Reason code + tagging

Mandatory Growth Fund reason code from day one, point-of-sale tagging wired up, and a daily deployment feed — so every deployment is attributable and the acquisition/retention split is visible.

5

Weekly scoreboard + clean baselines

Weekly report becomes pace-vs-run-rate only (no re-cut pay), and baselines re-lock on acquisition-only sales (they were ~4× too high on all-IFPs).

Full technical build-list — config keys, tables, functions and endpoints — is in the written recommendation.

The thing we must get right

Store managers confirm exactly who's in

The biggest thing to manage is who is and isn't included at each store. Entitlement is only as good as the roster it's locked from — so managers confirm it, in writing, as part of the briefing.

1

Pre-fill

Each store manager gets their current team list, pre-populated from our records — names and email addresses.

2

Confirm, add, remove

In the briefing, the manager ticks who's in, adds anyone missing (name + email), and removes anyone who shouldn't be — via a simple form / Teams link.

3

Lock

Confirmed rosters lock the entitlement list for the month. No confirmation, no go-live — it's the gate on the door.

How

A per-store confirmation form (Microsoft Form or a one-click link from the workbench, pre-filled and editable — no login needed), completed during the manager briefing and stamped against the store. It feeds the month-start entitlement lock directly.
