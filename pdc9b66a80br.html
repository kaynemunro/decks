Why · the competitive reality

Phone discounting is instantly matched.

We've proven it over years. A shelf-price cut is public — rivals mirror it within days. It's brilliant at moving volume between carriers and terrible at building loyalty.

Step 1

We cut the shelf price

The offer is advertised, public, and identical for everyone who walks in.

Step 2

Rivals match in days

Because it's visible, matching it is trivial. The playing field re-levels almost immediately.

Net result

Margin destruction, no share shift

Everyone pays more to stand still. Nobody becomes more loyal.

The conclusion we drew: we needed a smarter way to defend our own base and acquire from competitors' bases — one that can't simply be copied off a shelf.

The idea

Phone Dollars — value that's personal, not public.

A dollar-equivalent loyalty currency — 1 Phone Dollar = $1 — held in a customer's One Wallet. It's loaded dynamically and personalised, not advertised on a shelf. Because the value is targeted and private, it can't be matched like a headline discount.

Defend

Retention

We load value to our own customers who are most at risk of leaving — sized to the risk. The right amount, to the right person, at the right moment.

Attack

Acquisition

Competitors' customers who check whatsinmywallet.one.nz find a personalised balance already waiting for them — a reason to switch that only they can see.

Value customers can see and feel — real dollars towards the transaction that matters most: their next phone.

Where the value comes from

Four drivers, all observed in production.

~$4.8m/yr

Reduced churn on the engaged base

~200k engaged customers churn 4pp lower than matched control — roughly 8,000 customers retained a year at $600 annual margin each.

~$2.5m/yr

At-risk save campaigns

50–100k at-risk customers targeted monthly, delivering 200–500 saves a month — a mid-point of 4,200 saves a year at $600 each.

~$7.9m/yr

WhatsInMyWallet acquisition

$90k/month of media drives ~5,000 wallets and ~300 month-1 connections, doubling to ~550 over months 2–5. That's $660k of LTV per $90k spend — a 7.3× media ROI.

2×+ attach

Quality of acquired customers

Broadband attach >10% and second-mobile attach ~48% — over double standard acquisition. Multi-product cohorts churn at 3–4% versus 26% for mobile-only.
